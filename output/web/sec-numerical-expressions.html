    Example 1.1.7. Order of Operations.

    To evaluate an expression, we must follow the correct order of operations. These rules can be remembered by their acronym PEMDAS:
    1. Parentheses

    2. Exponents
      ðŸ”—

    3. Multiplication and Division (from left to right)
      ðŸ”—
      ðŸ”—
    4. Addition and Subtraction (from left to right)
      ðŸ”—
      ðŸ”—
    ðŸ”—
    Note: Multiplication does not always come before Division, and Addition does not always come before Subtraction. They are performed from left to right as they appear in the expression.
    ðŸ”—
    For example, when evaluating the expression
    \begin{equation*} 5 + 3^2 \cdot (4-1) \end{equation*}
    we follow the order of operations as shown below:
    \begin{align*} \amp 5 + 3^2 \cdot (4-1) =\\ \amp 5 + 3^2 \cdot 3 =\\ \amp 5 + 9 \cdot 3 =\\ \amp 5 + 27 =\\ \amp 32 \end{align*}
    First, we evaluate inside the parentheses. Next, we compute the exponent. Then, we multiply. Finally, we add and get the expressionâ€™s value of \(32\text{.}\)
